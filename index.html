Library cuts could be scaled back

Plans to slash opening hours and remove 100 jobs at the Library of Birmingham could be scaled back under a dramatic rescue plan being considered by the city council.

It’s emerged that council leaders are talking to the British Library about a partnership deal which could go some way to finding the £1.5 million the local authority says it needs.

Brum on over: the London exodus to UK’s second city

According to an article by The Guardian, Birmingham's canals, culture and housing are all factors in helping make our fair city the top choice for people leaving the capital.
