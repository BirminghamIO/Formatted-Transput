Help Make Impact Hub Birmingham a Reality!

Impact Hub Birmingham, a collaborative workspace in Digbeth, aims to be "home to talent from a variety of sectors, a space where knowledge can be freely shared". But they need your help.

After signing a deal with their new landlord on Wednesday, they've started a Kickstarter Campaign to raise £50,000 needed to make this vision a reality.

Birmingham.IO are in Koding's Global Hackathon

This weekend koding.com are hosting the world’s first global hackathon. Out of the 19,814 teams to apply, 2,000 have been successful in getting a place in the competition, of which one is formed of members from Birmingham.IO.

Made up of TajinderSD, wprk, omarqureshi, King_Jk, and myself, we’re building a web app which will exercise your body as well as your mind. Keep an eye on the thread for updates.

Birmingham gets an Open Device Lab

On the 15th December Anthony and Alison of Ballyhoo will be throwing open the doors of the Longbridge Device Lab. Stocked full of devices ready to be used for testing websites and applications, they’d like to invite people to come and use the facility.

If you’ve got a drawer full of old devices that you’d like to give a better life to, maybe think about donating them, as your donation will be grateful received and will put them to good use.
