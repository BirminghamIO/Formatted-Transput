Innovation Birmingham Device Lab Opens

It seems that device labs are a lot like buses; you wait ages for one to arrive, then two come along at the same time. Hot on the heels of the Longbridge Open Device lab featured in our first newsletter, Innovation Birmingham have just launched their own.

Unlike the offering from Longbridge, which is an Open device lab (meaning anyone can use it for free), Innovation's lab is only available to it's members, with membership prices starting at £40 per month.

Packt Publishing Karaoke Night

On the 6th of February (psst, that's next Friday) Packt Publishing would like to gather all the members of the Birmingham tech scene in one place, and subject them to bad singing - that's right, they're hosting a Karaoke party.

Located on the second floor of Livery Place on Livery Street (just opposite Snow Hill), it kicks off at 7pm. Attendees will be plied with drinks, canapés, and plenty of additional entertainment. You can RSVP here, and I look forward to seeing you then (even if I'm not looking forward to hearing you).

Hi-tech gadgets to protect cyclists

Carmaker Jaguar Land Rover is aiming to prevent death on the roads, and make travel safer for everyone, by developing hi-tech warnings to protect cyclists and motorbike drivers from oncoming cars.

The Midlands manufacturer has developed new ‘Bike Sense’ technology, which will ‘tap’ the car driver on the shoulder and ring a bicycle bell to help prevent accidents involving bicycles and motorbikes.

Combine this with the council's Big Birmingham Bikes initiative, which among other things, will supply 5,000 free bicycles to residents, this is a great time to be a cyclist.
